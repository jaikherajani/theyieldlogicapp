# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 28 March 2026**

Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains how YieldLogic handles data, where it is stored, and what rights you have.

---

## 2. The Developer Does Not Collect Your Data

**YieldLogic does not collect, receive, transmit, or store any personal or financial data you enter into the App on any developer-owned server, database, or infrastructure of any kind.**

All values, figures, property names, scenarios, and settings you enter into the App are entered voluntarily by you and are stored exclusively within your own Apple ecosystem — on your device and, if you have iCloud enabled, in your own private iCloud account. This data belongs to you and remains entirely under your control at all times. The developer has no technical means to access, view, modify, or retrieve it.

This accurately reflects the App's privacy nutrition label on the App Store, which indicates that the developer does not collect data from users.

**What you enter — and where it stays:**

The following categories of data may be entered by you voluntarily into the App. None of it is transmitted to or accessible by the developer:

- Property names, addresses, and financial figures you enter into the App
- Scenario names, income rates, expense figures, and mortgage details
- Your preferred market, currency, and app settings

All of the above is stored locally on your device via Apple's SwiftData framework and, if iCloud sync is enabled on your device, in your private iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit framework.

**Sign In with Apple:**

The App uses Sign In with Apple for authentication. When you sign in, Apple provides the App with an opaque, anonymous user identifier issued by Apple. This identifier is used solely to authenticate your session and associate your locally stored data with your Apple ID on-device. It is not transmitted to, stored on, or processed by any developer-owned server. The developer does not receive your name or email address unless you explicitly choose to share them via Apple's consent prompt, and even if shared, this information is not stored or used by the developer beyond the authentication flow.

**We do not collect:**
- Location data
- Device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV)
- Any advertising identifiers
- Crash reports or analytics sent to any third-party service (e.g. Firebase, Mixpanel, Google Analytics)

**Apple's role in analytics:**
If you have opted in to share app analytics with developers in your iOS Settings, Apple may provide the developer with anonymous, aggregated metrics such as crash reports and session counts through App Store Connect. This data is fully anonymised and controlled by Apple — the developer has no ability to identify individual users from it.

## 3. How Your Data Is Stored

All property analyses, scenarios, and financial data you create are stored:

- Locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework
- In your private iCloud account (if iCloud sync is enabled on your device), using Apple's CloudKit framework

The developer does not operate any servers, databases, or cloud infrastructure of their own. Your financial data never leaves the Apple ecosystem and is never accessible to the developer.

In addition, photos and documents you attach to property income and expense records are stored in a folder in your iCloud Drive, visible in the Files app under **iCloud Drive → YieldLogic**. This folder belongs to you and is fully under your control. The App does not delete files from this folder when you delete property records — you manage these files directly.

Your Sign In with Apple credentials are stored securely in your device's Keychain. The developer cannot access your Keychain.

## 4. How Your Data Is Used

Your data is used solely to:
- Display your property analyses and scenarios within the App
- Perform hypothetical financial calculations on your device
- Sync your data across your own Apple devices via iCloud

The developer does not use your data for advertising, profiling, tracking, or any purpose beyond operating the App for you locally on your device.

## 8. Data Retention and Deletion

Your data is retained on your device and iCloud account for as long as you use the App or until you delete it. The developer holds no copy of your data and therefore cannot delete it on your behalf.

**To delete individual items:**
- Delete properties or scenarios directly within the App.

**To delete all data and close your account:**
- Go to Settings → Account → "Delete Account & Data". This permanently removes all properties, scenarios, and settings from your device and iCloud. This cannot be undone.
- **Note:** Attachment files (photos and documents) stored in your iCloud Drive under Files app → YieldLogic are intentionally not deleted by this action — they are your personal files and you retain full control over them. To remove them, delete the YieldLogic folder manually in Files.app or via [icloud.com](https://www.icloud.com).

**To remove iCloud data manually (if you have already uninstalled the App):**
- Go to iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Storage → YieldLogic → Delete Data.

**To revoke Sign In with Apple access:**
- Go to iOS Settings → [Your Name] → Password & Security → Sign in with Apple → YieldLogic → Stop Using Apple ID.

**If any data appears to remain after following the steps above:**

In rare cases, iCloud may retain file caches or metadata briefly after deletion. If you believe residual data is still present after uninstalling the App and completing the steps above, you can:

1. Visit [icloud.com](https://www.icloud.com), sign in, and check "iCloud Drive" for any YieldLogic folder — delete it if present.
2. Contact Apple Support directly at [apple.com/support](https://www.apple.com/support/) to request removal of any remaining data from iCloud infrastructure associated with this App's private container. Apple controls the underlying iCloud storage and can assist with complete removal upon request.

The developer does not have access to your iCloud container and cannot perform this removal on your behalf.

## 9. Your Rights

Because all your data is stored locally on your device and in your own iCloud account, you already have full and direct control over it at all times. The developer does not hold a copy of your data on any server and therefore cannot access, modify, or delete it on your behalf.

- **To access your data:** it is available directly within the App.
- **To correct your data:** edit or delete entries directly in the App.
- **To delete your data:** see Section 8 above.
- **To export your data:** Your data is stored directly on your device and in your private iCloud account — you already hold the only copy. This inherently satisfies your right to data portability under applicable data protection law. Machine-readable export within the App is not currently available.

If you have a question about your data that is not answered here, you may contact us (see Section 12), though our ability to assist is limited given we hold no copy of your data.

## 10. Security

We take reasonable technical measures to protect your data:

- All data is stored in Apple's managed frameworks (SwiftData, CloudKit, Keychain), which use industry-standard encryption
- iCloud data is encrypted in transit and at rest by Apple
- Keychain entries are encrypted by the device's secure enclave

No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.
